How Macros Are Calculated

The science behind calorie density and preset ratios.

4
kcal
Protein — 4 kcal per gram
Protein is the body's primary building material for muscle, enzymes, and hormones. The ISSN & ACSM recommend 1.6–2.2g/kg/day for active individuals. Our presets use body weight in lbs as the target gram intake — a practical rule-of-thumb aligned with this range. Normal (0.8g/lb ≈ 1.76g/kg) suits active lifestyles. High (1.0g/lb ≈ 2.2g/kg) is the athletic gold standard. Ultra-High (1.4g/lb ≈ 3.1g/kg) is validated for muscle preservation during aggressive caloric deficits by clinical trials.
4
kcal
Carbohydrates — 4 kcal per gram
Carbohydrates are the body's preferred fuel source, especially for the brain and high-intensity exercise. High Carb (~65%) is ideal for endurance athletes to maximise glycogen stores. Regular Carb (~50%) is consistent with standard dietary guidelines (USDA, WHO). Low Carb (~15%) shifts the body toward fat oxidation — beneficial for metabolic flexibility, insulin sensitivity, and sustained energy without blood sugar spikes.
9
kcal
Fats — 9 kcal per gram
Dietary fat is calorie-dense (9 kcal/g — more than double protein or carbs) and essential for fat-soluble vitamin absorption (A, D, E, K), hormone synthesis, and cellular membrane integrity. High Fat (~50%) is used in ketogenic and low-carb approaches where fat becomes the primary fuel. Low Fat (~20%) aligns with cardiovascular risk reduction protocols or high-carb endurance phases.
